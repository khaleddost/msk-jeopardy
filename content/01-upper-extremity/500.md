explanation: >
  On the **lateral** view, a **lunate dislocation** shows the lunate tipped
  volarly out of its fossa — the **"spilled teacup"** sign — while the
  **capitate remains colinear with the radius**. In a **perilunate**
  dislocation the lunate stays seated in the radius and the capitate is
  displaced dorsally. Both lie on the Mayfield spectrum of progressive carpal
  instability.
