  The **scaphoid** is the most commonly fractured carpal bone. Because its blood
  supply is **retrograde** (entering distally), **waist** and proximal-pole
  fractures risk **avascular necrosis** of the proximal pole. Snuffbox
  tenderness with normal initial films still warrants immobilization and
  follow-up imaging or MRI.
