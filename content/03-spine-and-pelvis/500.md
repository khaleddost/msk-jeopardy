options:
  A: Clay-shoveler fracture
  B: Extension teardrop fracture
  C: Flexion teardrop fracture
  D: Chance fracture
explanation: >
  A **flexion teardrop** fracture is a severe, **unstable** injury: a triangular
  anteroinferior body fragment, **posterior retropulsion** of the body into the
  canal, and disrupted posterior elements, carrying a high rate of **anterior
  cord syndrome**. Contrast it with the **extension teardrop** (a stable avulsion
  at the anteroinferior corner, classically C2, from hyperextension).
